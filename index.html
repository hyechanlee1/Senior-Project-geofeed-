Validation, Adoption and Accuracy of Geofeeds

Project Vision

This research aims to investigate the validation, adoption, and accuracy of Geofeeds as a potential solution for improving IP geolocation. Geofeeds are self-published lists of IP address ranges linked to geographic data, offering the possibility of more accurate location information compared to traditional methods. The validation phase will address key questions related to the compliance of Geofeed data with the RFC8805 standard, the types of errors present in invalidated data, the proportions of these errors, the IP address ranges covered by the validated data, and the Autonomous System Numbers (ASNs) from which invalidated data originates. By answering these questions, the research will assess the overall reliability of Geofeed data and identify areas for improvement.

The adoption phase will focus on understanding the current use of Geofeeds in the industry. Key questions will explore which IP address ranges are managed and allocated by each Regional Internet Registry (RIR), which organizations have published Geofeed data, and the differences in adoption rates between IPv4 and IPv6 prefixes. Additionally, the research will examine which types of organizations, such as Internet Service Providers (ISPs), content providers, and transit providers, are most active in publishing Geofeed data. These findings will provide insight into the factors influencing the adoption of Geofeeds and their integration into the broader geolocation ecosystem.

The accuracy testing phase will evaluate the effectiveness of Geofeeds in providing precise IP geolocation data. The research will focus on identifying the most effective metrics for assessing accuracy, such as round-trip time (RTT) thresholds and geographical distance offsets, and will analyze the distribution of inaccuracies across organizationsâ€™ Geofeeds. The accuracy of the information provided in Geofeed data will be compared to traditional geolocation methods, determining whether Geofeeds can reduce errors and provide more reliable location information. These insights will help guide future geolocation standards and the broader adoption of Geofeeds in various industries.
